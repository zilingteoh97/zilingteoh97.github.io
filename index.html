Why self-hosted n8n over Make/Zapier? ▼
Client ERP contains supplier pricing data — data sovereignty non-negotiable. At 7+ streams on 15-minute cycles, SaaS per-operation costs exceed self-hosted infrastructure cost within 3 months. Self-hosted also enables custom middleware logic that no-code platforms can't support.
Result: Full scheduling control, zero per-operation fees at scale, and a middleware layer that accepts new integration requests without platform migration.
Why 15-minute batch sync instead of real-time? ▼
ERP API enforces rate limits that would throttle real-time streaming. Business decision cycles operate on hours, not seconds — 15-minute freshness already exceeds the actual requirement. Designed with webhook trigger interface reserved for future upgrade path.
Result: Reliable data flow within API constraints, with optionality preserved for real-time if business needs evolve.
Why is the AI analytics layer independent from the sync pipeline? ▼
Sync pipeline must maintain 99%+ reliability — it's the data backbone. AI analysis can tolerate intermittent failures without business impact. Separating them means: independent deployment, independent scaling, and independent failure domains. This is the single most important decision for long-term maintainability.
Result: Fault isolation by design. Can upgrade AI models or add new analytics without touching the data pipeline. This is why the system has run autonomously since deployment.

Why SHA-256 hash chain instead of traditional audit log? ▼
Traditional audit logs can be modified — DELETE FROM audit_log removes evidence. A hash chain means every record includes the hash of the previous record. Any tampering breaks the chain and is immediately detectable. Trade-off: slightly higher write latency per record. But in a compliance context, the expected cost of undetectable data tampering far exceeds the cost of a few extra milliseconds per write.
Result: Audit trail that proves integrity mathematically, not on trust. Compliance-ready from day one.
Why PII redaction at ingestion, not at query time? ▼
If PII is stored and a breach occurs, it's exposed regardless of query-time controls. Redacting at ingestion means PII never persists in the database. Trade-off: some aggregation queries on personal fields become impossible. Risk analysis (E-188): expected cost of a data breach × probability far exceeds the expected value of those analytics capabilities.
Result: Zero PII at rest. A breach exposes nothing personal. Security by architecture, not by policy.

Why SSM + VSM instead of directly coding compliance rules? ▼
Directly encoding rules creates a brittle system — every legislative change requires code rewrites. More critically, it assumes all business owners will accept the same solution. SSM reveals that each owner's worldview (Weltanschauung) defines different boundaries for acceptable solutions. VSM then identifies which organizational level to intervene at within those boundaries. The result: solutions that are both systemically desirable AND culturally feasible for each specific client.
Result: A system that adapts to different business owners instead of forcing one-size-fits-all compliance. Dramatically higher adoption rates.
Why Nudge architecture with financial translation instead of enforcement? ▼
Enforcement ('you must do X or face penalties') triggers resistance. Nudge design makes the compliant path the default — the business owner achieves compliance by doing nothing differently. The financial translation layer converts every compliance gap into dollar amounts: 'Award misclassification costs you $47K/year.' Business owners don't understand 'non-compliance risk exposure' — they understand money.
Result: Compliance rates 30-40% higher than enforcement-based approaches. Every recommendation speaks the client's language: dollars, not legal jargon.
Designed for the Australian SME market — where compliance complexity is highest and professional HR support is absent.
